API stands for Application Programmable Interface. An API acts as the middleman between the client and the server. I will use a very common analogy to try explaining it.
Let’s create a scenario of a popular restaurant that we are visiting. We see the menu and when we are done deciding the food items, we call upon a waiter to take the order. The waiter then notes down all the items we requested and hands over the items to the kitchen to be prepared. Upon the preparation of the items, the waiter is called in the kitchen to take down the items to us and serve it.
Here, we are the customers/client that places an order/request to the waiter/API for the items/query. The kitchen/server responds to the customers via API/waiter by preparing the dish and sending it through waiter/API.
